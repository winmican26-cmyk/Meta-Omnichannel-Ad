01Connect your Meta Business account

Click Get Started from the home page or the top nav. You'll be sent to Meta's standard Business Login screen where you choose which ad accounts and pages this engine can manage on your behalf.

What you'll be asked to approve: the ability to create and manage ad sets and ads, read insights for those campaigns, and switch between the ad accounts you authorise. Meta shows you each one before you accept.

Once connected, you'll land on the dashboard with your default ad account selected. To switch between accounts at any time, use the account picker in the top right.

Don't see your account? Make sure you're an admin or finance editor on the Business that owns the ad account in Meta Business Settings, then disconnect and reconnect.

02Create your first CCCO campaign

Open Campaigns → New CCCO Campaign. Fill in the essentials:

  • Name — internal label; can be edited later.
  • Daily budget — in minor units (cents). Meta enforces a minimum per currency.
  • Target event — the conversion event Meta optimises toward (e.g. PURCHASE, LEAD, COMPLETE_REGISTRATION).
  • Pixel ID — your Meta web pixel, owned by or shared with this ad account.
  • App ID — your Meta app, with App Events SDK or CAPI configured for the same event.
  • Web URL and optional iOS / Android deep links.
Safety net: every new ad set and ad is created as PAUSED. Nothing spends until you flip it to ACTIVE.

03Use the AI Optimizer

From any draft campaign, click Optimize. The engine returns:

  • A recommended web vs app budget split, with a reason for each weight.
  • Predicted CPA based on historical patterns.
  • A suggested bid cap and a deep-link routing rule.
  • A creative tip tailored to your event type.

Review the suggestion and apply with one click. The cost is 15 credits per call.

The optimizer is Claude-backed when ANTHROPIC_API_KEY is configured and falls back to the rule engine otherwise. Every call is logged for the Proof Report below.

03bRun the Proof Report (Claude vs. Rule Engine)

Once you have at least seven days of synced insights on a CCCO ad set, hit POST /optimize/backtest. The engine splits each owned ad set's history into a leak-free history window and a holdout window, then scores both optimizers' predictions against what actually happened. This tests whether Claude beats the baseline on your account — it does not assume the answer.

  • MAE and RMSE on predicted CPA per optimizer.
  • Per-adset wins on CPA and app-share.
  • An honest notes field that calls out rule-vs-rule comparisons when no Claude key is configured.

The backtest is strictly scoped to your (session_id, ad_account_id); it cannot read other accounts. Cost: 50 credits.

04Generate Meta-ready creatives

Open the Creative Studio, paste your campaign basics, and you'll receive one or more variant specs containing the omnichannel link spec, the right deep-link treatment, and optionally an Advantage+ Catalog template URL spec. Use the JSON directly when you create the ad, or copy values into Ads Manager.

Catalog mode: set catalog_mode: true with a product_id to get a template_url_spec for Advantage+ Shopping ads.

05Sync and read live insights

Click Sync Insights on any CCCO ad set. The engine pulls the last 30 days from Meta and stores daily rows. The dashboard then shows:

  • Total conversions and spend, with daily breakdown.
  • Channel split — web vs app share of conversions.
  • CCCO lift percent versus a same-event single-channel baseline.
  • Average CPA trend.

Sync costs 20 credits. You can re-sync as often as you want; rows are idempotent on (ad_set, date).

06Migrate legacy single-channel campaigns

Click Scan for Migration. The engine inspects your active campaigns and flags web-only and app-only candidates that are good CCCO upgrade targets. Each candidate includes the recommended event and an expected CPA lift estimate.

Pick a candidate, click Plan Migration, and the engine returns a ready-for-inputs CCCO structure — you fill the placeholder app, pixel, store URLs, and deep links, then create the upgraded campaign.

07Save and duplicate winning configurations

From any successful campaign, click Save as Template to capture the full CCCO configuration. Duplicate it later with a new name, new daily budget, and new target audience — useful for rolling out the same play across regions or product lines.

09Privacy & your data rights

OAuth tokens are encrypted at rest. We do not see or store your Meta password. To exercise your GDPR rights (access, rectification, erasure, portability, restriction, objection, withdrawal of consent), use the DSR form on the home page or email us at privacy@your-domain.example. We respond within one calendar month.

You can also disconnect this engine entirely from Meta Business Settings → Business Integrations at any time. Meta will notify us, we'll purge your tokens, and stage your downstream records for deletion.

10FAQ

Why is my new campaign PAUSED?
Intentional. Every new ad set and ad is created PAUSED so you can review the structure in Ads Manager before any spend. Flip to ACTIVE when you're ready.
Why does CCCO require both a pixel and an app ID?
CCCO ads are optimised on a single event that fires across both surfaces. Meta requires you to declare the matching event for both your pixel (web) and your app, so it can attribute conversions back to one optimisation goal. The custom_event_type must match across both entries.
My Meta App Secret is showing as empty in the dashboard. Where do I set it?
In your local .env file, set META_APP_SECRET to the value shown at App Settings → Basic → App Secret on the Meta developer dashboard. Restart the server after changing it.
Can I use a System User token instead of OAuth?
Yes — agencies and unattended automations can paste a long-lived System User token in the dashboard's "Advanced" tab. The engine will use it in place of the OAuth flow. Standard OAuth is still recommended for first-time setup.
What's the difference between "Limited" and "Full" access on the Marketing API?
Limited Access works only on ad accounts where you (the app admin) are an admin — useful for development. Full Access is required to serve other advertisers' accounts via OAuth and requires App Review plus Business Verification. See the App Review roadmap in our developer docs.
How do I revoke access?
Go to Meta Business Settings → Business Integrations, find this app, and click "Remove". Meta will send a signed deletion request to us; we will purge your stored tokens and stage your records for deletion. You'll receive a confirmation code you can use to check status.
The engine says "No active session" when I try to create a campaign.
Your OAuth session has expired (Meta long-lived tokens last ~60 days). Click Sign in again to refresh. Future versions will refresh proactively before expiry.
